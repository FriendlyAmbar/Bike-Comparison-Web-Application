The Street Rod is a new addition to Harley-Davidson’s Dark Custom line-up. Based on the Street 750, the Street Rod
gets several visual upgrades and a more powerful engine to give it the sporty appeal of a street bike. The 
flyscreen, flat drag-style handlebar, short tail section and the new alloy wheels set the Street Rod apart from
its host. The Street Rod is propelled by the same 749cc liquid-cooled V-twin engine as the Street 750. However,
it gets a larger air box, a new throttle body, revised cylinder heads with high-lift camshafts and a stubby 
exhaust. Even the compression ratio has been increased, translating in a higher output of 70bhp and 64Nm of 
torque. The engine is mated to a six-speed transmission.
The Street Rod is available in three paint schemes – olive gold, matte grey and black.

The Hyosung GV650 Aquila Pro is a laidback cruiser with enough firepower to challenge sports motorcycles.
As a cruiser, its performance is sensational and can surpass that of more expensive rivals; fuel efficiency
suffers. The Aquila Pro650 gets a 647cc 8-valve engine that produces 74bhp and 62Nm of torque. It is belt 
driven and gets a five-speed gearbox. The motorcycle is built on a perimeter tubular steel cradle and gets
upside down telescopic front suspension and hydraulic double shock absorbers at the rear.  Hyosung has got 
the looks spot on with those beautiful swee ping lines and have not overdone it with too much chrome 
detailing. It is available in three colours shadow black, lava red and phantom silver.

The Hyosung ST7 gets a swept-back design, massive fenders, a huge fuel tank, loads of chrome, a simple round 
headlamp and step-up seats. The ST7 gets an analogue speedometer with a multi-function digital display. This 
instrument cluster is positioned on the fuel tank and because of the laidback riding position, gives the rider
easy access to all the information. It is powered by a four-stroke 678cc 90 degree V-twin engine which 
produces 58bhp and 57Nm of torque. The water-cooled engine is mated to a five-speed constant mesh gearbox with
a wet multiplate clutch. The engine gets electronic fuel injection to make it a more reliable. The Hyosung ST7
is available in three paint schemes- red, black and white.

The Iron 883 is an entry-level cruiser and is assembled in India. It is a raw, stripped-down, aggressive bike 
that gives the rider an authentic, throwback riding experience, and at a very aggressive price point. The Iron 
883 gets an 883cc 4-valve engine that produces 50bhp and 69Nm of torque. It gets a six-speed gearbox that 
transmits the power to the rear wheel via transmission belt. It is 2245mm long, 820mm wide, 1120mm tall and 
has a wheel base of 1510mm. The motorcycle tips the scales at 260kg. It is available in three colours vivid 
olive gold, charcoal denim and black denim.

The engine on the Bonneville Bobber is of 1200cc, liquid-cooled, parallel-twin motor that produces 80bhp and 105Nm
of torque. But the power and torque curves on the Bobber are different; electronic updates and a new dual air 
filter design will make the Bobber produce more torque and power lower down in the rev range than the T120. The 
Bobber gets a hard-tail (no rear suspension) look, thanks to a new cage-type swingarm and a monoshock which is 
mounted underneath the seat. The front fork is updated too, and is shorter than the T120. The solo seat is 
adjustable in two positions - low and back, or high and forward. The new Bonneville Bobber may not appeal to 
every kind of riders, but there will be some who will find its looks irresistible, particularly those with an 
affinity for custom cruisers.

The new Scout introduces Indian’s first-ever liquid-cooled engine. Producing 100bhp of power and a healthy 97Nm
of torque, the new liquid cooled V-twin engine promises to offer an unmatched riding experience thanks to its
electronic fuel injection, counter-balancing and a 6-speed gearbox.The Indian Scout is a small bike for a
cruiser, but it's fast and keeps you entertained, whether you're out on a twisty highway or rolling through
suburban traffic. The instrumentations include a digital tachometer, odometer, trip metre, engine temperature
read-out and low fuel lamp. The all-new Scout is available in four colours – red, silver and two shades of
black.
